About

EcoMapper is an online mapping platform for people with little technical background in remote sensing. We developed EcoMapper to enable you to map and report on the changing status of ecosystems, contributing to a global effort to assess the status of all ecosystems on Earth by the year 2025.

Assessment

We know quite a lot about which species around the world are most endangered. The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species identifies species that are most at risk of extinction. But scientists have become increasingly concerned that the habitats of species and the ecological processes that influence the relationships between species are not adequately considered.

The newly developed IUCN Red List of Ecosystems identifies ecosystems as Critically Endangered, Endangered, Vulnerable or Least Concern. Together, the Red Lists for species and ecosystems will provide a more comprehensive view of the status of the environment and its biodiversity than either can on its own.

Mapping ecosystems in Remap is a crucial step in conducting an ecosystem assessment. Find out more about the IUCN Red List of Ecosystems: www.iucnrle.org

Very high level overview

What are Classes?

In order to make a map of your ecosystem EcoMapper needs data to train a model called a classifier. We take data points you provide to us and intersect those points with various satellite datasets to create a dataset that we then use to predict what the rest of the map is.

Workflow

The following is a general tutorial to guide you through making a map of your ecosystem.

1. Select region of Interest

Use the search bar to bring the map to your region and then click Focus Region to bring the polygon onto the map.

Manipulate the polygon so that it encapsulates the general region of your ecosystem.

2. Build Training set

In the sidebar click Build Training Set and click the gear next to the highlighted class.

You can then relabel your ecosystem and change its colour.

When you are happy with its name and colour click OK

Add a couple more classes eg. Water, Agriculture, ... suggestions?

You can then begin adding training points.

Click Add Markers

Clicking on the map will now add a marker that we will use as data to train the model which builds the map

You should aim for 40+ for each class.

3. Select Predictors

In this section you can choose which predictors you want to use in the classification. More information about each predictor can be found in the methods Section

4. Classify

Click Classify to make a map, this can take up to a minute.

When the classification has completed you should be able to explore your map

If you add or remove markers or add or remove predictors you can rerun your classification by pressing Reclassify

5. Results

In this section you can view various classification metrics as well as look at an area histogram.

6. Export

In this section you can download your training points as a CSV for future reuse or Log in with your Google Account and then download the map as an image to your Google Drive.
